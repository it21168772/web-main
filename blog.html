GALLE | BENTOTA DAY TOUR The Galle fort is a world heritage site and the largest remaining fortress in Asia built by European occupiers.

The most enjoyable way to experience the Galle Fort is by walking. A leisurely walk leads past the old Dutch Church, the Governor's house, the spice warehouses, Court Square, the Kacheri (town hall), the lighthouse and on to the sea wall and ramparts.

Colombo, the vibrant capital of Sri Lanka, weaves a captivating tapestry of history and modernity. With a heritage influenced by Portuguese, Dutch, and British colonial rulers, the city showcases colonial buildings alongside contemporary skyscrapers and bustling shopping malls. MaduRiver Bort Safari
MaduRiver | Bort Safari Will take you through a lavish mangrove forest within Madu Ganga

Madu River, nestled near the charming town of Balapitiya along the Colombo-Galle highway, is a captivating wetland sanctuary. Here, the river gracefully merges with the estuary, creating a breathtaking natural spectacle. As the river meanders through the landscape, it weaves a tapestry of picturesque islands, forming a unique and vibrant ecosystem. Visitors can embark on delightful boat rides offered by nearby hotels, allowing them to explore the intricate network of canals that wind through this pristine paradise. Additionally, a plethora of accommodation and dining options are available, ensuring a comfortable and indulgent experience. A leisurely excursion along the serene waters of Madu River offers the chance to immerse oneself in the stunning beauty of the southern coast, where verdant mangrove forests teem with diverse birdlife and fascinating reptilian inhabitants. It's a place where you can savor a laid-back escape while indulging in a variety of engaging activities that celebrate the wonders of nature.

Udawalawe National Park
Travel | Park An Epic Elephant Safari in Udawalawe National Park in SriLanka

Udawalawe National Park Established during 1972, with a dry land of area 119 square miles. It generally has a very humid but warm climate, very famourable for most species that live there. It is home to even plants such as Satin trees, ebony , etc. However the main attraction is the elephants of which this reserve is very famous for. The endangered Sambar deer and leopards along with a lot of mammals can be seen while on Safari. Birdwatching is done here to see the Sri lankan spurfowl an endemic species along with a lot of other birds.
